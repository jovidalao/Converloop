Arrow Lines As Dialogue Shape

The arrows are no longer wrapped around a chat symbol. Their own stroke paths suggest the outline, tail, and exchange of a dialogue bubble.

1. Bubble Contour Arrows

The two arrows trace the top and bottom of a dialogue bubble. The small lower bend is part of the return stroke, not an extra symbol.

Recommended: clearest answer to “arrow line shape forms dialogue.”

2. Tail Turn Arrows

Closer to the original A1 loop, with only a small tail-like turn in the return line. It is simpler, but the dialogue cue is more subtle.

Tradeoff: cleaner, less immediately “conversation.”

3. Message Edge Arrows

The arrow strokes flatten into message-line edges. This is very minimal, but it reads more as text/message flow than a dialogue bubble.

Tradeoff: highly simple, weaker speech-bubble silhouette.
