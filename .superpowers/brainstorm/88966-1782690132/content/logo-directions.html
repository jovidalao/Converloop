Converloop Logo Directions

Pick the direction that feels closest. The sketches are intentionally rough: this is about concept and brand tone, not final polish.

A. Conversation Loop

Two reply paths form a loop around one learning moment. Best if the brand should feel direct, active, and easy to recognize as a chat app.

Good for: app icon, sidebar mark, favicon, simple monochrome version.

B. Memory Thread

A conversation line becomes a retained learning trace. Best if the brand should emphasize local memory, progress, and long-term coaching.

Good for: product storytelling, learning data, coach panel, premium-feeling desktop identity.

C. Desktop Coach

A correction bubble inside a desktop window. Best if the brand should immediately communicate “language tutor app” over a more abstract identity.

Good for: onboarding, docs, screenshots, users who need the product category to be obvious.

My recommendation

Start from A, borrow the memory warmth from B, and avoid making C the primary logo unless clarity matters more than distinctiveness. A compact looped conversation mark fits the name Converloop and can scale down cleanly.
